<h3>💡 Industry Talk Reflection — iZeno</h3>
<p>The iZeno session pushed the technical frontier of Data &amp; AI further than any other talk this semester. The team mapped out where enterprise AI is actually heading &mdash; past static analytics, into autonomous and semantic systems that plan, retrieve, and reason in real time.</p>

<p><strong>🚀 Technical Insights</strong></p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Agentic AI &amp; Multimodal AI.</strong> Systems that don't just process data, but autonomously plan, execute, and evaluate actions across text, audio, and video inputs.</li>
	<li><strong>RAG &amp; Knowledge Mining.</strong> How enterprises solve the hallucination problem of standard LLMs by securely anchoring open-source or proprietary models to their own data &mdash; no retraining required.</li>
	<li><strong>Model Context Protocol (MCP).</strong> The connector layer that lets LLMs safely reach into private, enterprise-specific data sources.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>⚙️ The Production Reality Check</strong></p>
<p>The most impactful realisation was the gap between classroom data projects and real production environments. In academia I chase model accuracy on clean datasets. In production, that is barely half the story.</p>

<blockquote><em>"Technical skills alone are not enough. In production, engineering realities &mdash; scalability, data quality, continuous monitoring, and strict security &amp; governance &mdash; matter just as much as, if not more than, the core AI logic."</em></blockquote>

<p>Without data governance and quality frameworks, even the most advanced conversational BI or RAG system becomes a liability rather than an enterprise asset.</p>

<p><strong>🎯 What I'm Taking Away</strong></p>
<p>This session fundamentally changed how I view my own path as a data engineering student. I now look at pipelines through an operational lens &mdash; data drift, scaling bottlenecks, security boundaries, and observability are first-class concerns, not afterthoughts. iZeno has motivated me to dig deeper into enterprise orchestration tools and data quality frameworks so the systems I build are production-ready, not just demo-ready.</p>

<p>🙏 Sincere thanks to the iZeno team for bridging the gap between academic theory and production-grade engineering.</p>
