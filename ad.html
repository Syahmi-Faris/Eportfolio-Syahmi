WHAT IS APPLICATION DEVELOPMENT ?

Application Development is about going from idea to a working software product. The course covers modern application architecture, requirements gathering, proposal writing, and full product design with a team. In this semester I worked on DevSync, a group product where we defined the product vision, requirements, and high-level architecture from scratch, and delivered both a written proposal and a working lab assignment.

DevSync — eHadir: Smart Attendance & Discipline Management System

A cross-platform mobile application built with Flutter and Firebase, designed for educational institutions to streamline class scheduling, track student attendance in real time, and manage discipline reports. Features role-based access for lecturers, program heads, and department heads, with support for conflict-aware booking, attendance logging, and severity-tagged discipline reporting.

Reflection

AD pushed me to think about software from the user's perspective. Working on the DevSync proposal with my team made me realise how much of product development is about deciding what NOT to build — scope discipline matters as much as engineering quality.

Looking back, the proposal-writing skills and architecture-thinking from this course are immediately useful in any team I will join. The course taught me that good software starts with a clear product story.
